# krunch — design system

## Design Context

### Users
Technically-literate people (developers, researchers, decision-makers) who convene a panel of LLMs to deliberate a hard question and want a considered verdict — not a single model's take. They run multi-round sessions at a desk, often watching a live deliberation unfold over minutes. They value seeing *how* the panel reasoned, not just the answer.

### Brand Personality
Weighty · warm · deliberate. krunch is a **deliberation chamber**, not a chat app. It should feel like convening a jury in a dim, candle-warm room: gravitas, patience, and the sense that a real body of voices is weighing something. Three words: *considered, physical, old-world*.

### Aesthetic Direction — "Deliberation Chamber"
- **Theme: dark**, warm-tinted charcoal (a chamber at night), never cool/blue.
- **Accent: aged brass / amber** — candlelight, brass fittings, a gavel.
- **Signature moment:** the room literally **warms and brightens as the panel converges** — a low amber glow behind the chamber that intensifies with mean confidence, cooling toward ember-red on deadlock.
- **Seats around a table:** panelists are physical seats; the mediator sits at the head.
- **The verdict is a ruling** — typeset with authority, exportable as a filing.
- Anti-references: generic AI dark-mode (cyan-on-black, purple gradients, glassmorphism), shadcn-default indigo/zinc, sterile SaaS dashboards.

### Type
- **Display / gravitas:** Young Serif — warm, carved, old-world; used large (wordmark, verdict title, ruling states).
- **Body / UI:** Hanken Grotesk — humanist, warm, excellent at UI sizes.
- **Telemetry:** Fragment Mono — seat ids, confidence %, round counters, model names.
- (Deliberately NOT Inter/DM/Fraunces/Playfair/Cormorant/Space/IBM Plex.)

### Design Principles
1. **The room is alive.** State is atmosphere — warmth, glow, and light track the deliberation, not just badges.
2. **Gravitas over chrome.** Weight comes from type, space, and restraint, not drop-shadows and gradients.
3. **Everyone has a seat.** Agents are physical presences at a table, each with a voice you can watch.
4. **A verdict is a record.** Outcomes read as authored rulings and filed transcripts, not toast notifications.
5. **Calm under load.** Characterful but legible across long multi-round sessions; motion serves comprehension.
